\subsection{Detailed Description}
The \doxyref{Transport\-Junction}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transport_junction} is the place where {\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}} s pick up the goods to move them around in a manufacturing system. The goods are normally modeled as some kind of {\ttfamily Sim\-Process} es. So what happens at the \doxyref{Transport\-Junction}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transport_junction} is some kind of Rendez-\/vous synchronisation, where {\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}} s meet the goods to carry them around. There is one wait queue for the masters ({\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}}s) and one queue for the slaves ({\ttfamily Sim\-Process} es), where they have to wait for each other to cooperate. The {\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}} s are the masters which perform the transportation. The corporate transportation process is described in the method {\ttfamily cooperation} in a subclass of {\ttfamily \doxyref{Transportation}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transportation}}. The goods ({\ttfamily Sim\-Process} es) transported as slaves keep still during the transport and will be reactivated thereafter. The main difference to a normal {\ttfamily Wait\-Queue} is, that in this case one master ({\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}}) can cooperate with more than one slave process at a time. The number of slaves processes which will be transported togehter will be determined by the master {\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}} process. It depends on the number of available slaves and will be between the min\-Load and the capacity of the {\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}}. Note that a {\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}} with a large minimum\-Load in front of the wait queue might block all following {\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}} s in the queue until enough goods are available to satisfy his minimum load. Use different priorities for the different {\ttfamily \doxyref{Transporter}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transporter}} s to cope with this problem. Or set the pass\-By flag to {\ttfamily true} to make it possible for transporters with a low minimum\-Load to pass by the other transporters. Use the methods {\ttfamily \doxyref{set\-Pass\-By()}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transport_junction_aeebcbec5f1690a85811b86b37c3755fa}} and {\ttfamily \doxyref{get\-Pass\-By()}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transport_junction_a2daffded1a01863da48b3db2ea9b76e0}}. The first sort criteria for the queues is always highest priorities first, the second queueing discipline of the underlying queues and the capacity limit can be determined by the user (default is F\-I\-F\-O and unlimited capacity). \doxyref{Transport\-Junction}{p.}{classdesmoj_1_1extensions_1_1application_domains_1_1production_1_1_transport_junction} is derived from {\ttfamily Wait\-Queue} which in turn is derived from {\ttfamily Queue\-Based}, which provides all the statistical functionality for the queues.
